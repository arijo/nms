Privacy and Terms of Use

Sony DADC US Inc. d/b/a New Media Solutions (“Sony”) is strongly committed to protecting the privacy of those who entrust us with their personal information. With regard to personal information, Sony respects the privacy of our clients and our clients’ customers and all those who do business with us.

In furtherance of our commitment, Sony complies with the U.S. – E.U. Safe Harbor Framework and the U.S. - Swiss Safe Harbor Framework as set forth by the U.S. Department of Commerce regarding the collection, use, and retention of data from the European Union and Switzerland respectively. Sony has certified its compliance with respect to personal information that we process. Accordingly, Sony adheres to the respective sets of Safe Harbor Privacy Principles and Frequently Asked Questions, as agreed to by the U.S. Department of Commerce and the European Commission, as well as those agreed to by the U.S. Department of Commerce and the Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner of Switzerland. Information regarding both Safe Harbor Frameworks can be found at: http://export.gov/safeharbor.

Scope

This Safe Harbor Privacy Policy ("Policy") sets forth the privacy principles that Sony follows when handling any personal information collected or received for the purposes of conducting its business, where such personal information is transferred from its EU clients to the United States. Sony’s business, for purposes of this Policy, includes the collection of data by Sony on behalf of its clients through online and other electronic methods connected to the online environment, such as network connected Blu-ray players. Such collection of data is conducted through its services now marketed as Digital Copy, BD-Live, and Creative Services and through various client-labeled or “white-labeled” websites.

Collection of Information

Both personal information and non-personal information are collected by Sony on behalf of its clients. This information is collected through active and passive means.

Examples of personal information collected are name, email address, opt-in preferences and where applicable mailing address and date of birth.

Examples of passive information collected are certain information automatically collected and stored in log files. This information includes internet protocol (IP) addresses, browser type, internet service provider (ISP), referring/exit pages, operating system, date/time stamp, and clickstream data.

This information, which does not identify individual users, is used on behalf of Sony’s clients to analyze trends, to administer the sites, to track users’ movements around the sites and to gather demographic information about the user base as a whole.

Sony may use cookies on its sites to collect information if requested by its clients. Cookies are small text file that are stored on a user’s computer for record-keeping purposes. Sony may link the information stored in cookies to other personal information that has been collected.

If requested by its clients, Sony may also use a third-party tracking service that uses cookies, log files, scripts and other tracking technologies to track non-personally identifiable information about visitors to its sites in the aggregate to collect usage and volume statistics.

Public Forums

The sites built may offer publicly accessible blogs or community forums. Personal information may be posted on these forums by users. Users should be made aware that any information provided in these areas may be read, collected, and used by others who access them.

Notice

As an agent processing personal information under the direction of its clients, Sony has no direct relationship with the individuals whose personal information it processes. Sony works with its clients to help them provide notice of data processing to individuals, including information concerning (1) the purposes for which personal information is collected and used; (2) a contact person to whom inquiries or complaints may be directed; (3) the types of third parties to whom personal information is disclosed; and (4) the choices and means that individuals are offered for limiting use and disclosure of personal information.

Choice

As an agent processing personal information under the direction of its clients, Sony has no direct relationship with the individuals whose personal information it processes. Sony may disclose personal information to third party service providers in the course of processing personal information such as a hosting service provider.

At the direction of its clients, Sony works with its clients to help them inform individuals about the possibility of such disclosures and helps them provide individuals with the choice of opting-out of the database(s) on which processing is performed. Sony only processes personal information in accordance with instructions of its clients, and only for purposes that are compatible with those for which it was originally collected or subsequently authorized by the individual. Sony does not process any sensitive personal information, and only processes personal information that has been collected and provided by its clients through the web where the individual expressly provides consent for their personal information to be used for marketing purposes.

Onward Transfer

As an agent processing personal information under the direction of its clients, Sony may transfer personal information to subsequent third party data controllers or processors when directed by its clients in the course of conducting business. Transfers to subsequent third parties are covered by the provisions in this Policy regarding notice and choice. Sony has confirmed that its third party data processors subscribe to the Safe Harbor Principles or has concluded agreements with these third parties requiring that they provide at least the same level of privacy protection as do the Safe Harbor Principles.

Security

Sony offers generally accepted standards of data security to protect personal information from loss, misuse and unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration and destruction. As an agent processing personal information under the direction of its clients, Sony has concluded a contract with its clients specifying the conditions under which personal information received from the EU are processed and kept secure. Sony has confirmed that its third party data processors subscribe to the Safe Harbor Principles or has appropriate contractual language in place with third party data processors providing that they must apply the Safe Harbor Principles to the processing of personal information received from Sony.

Data Integrity

Sony only processes personal information that is relevant to the services it provides, and only for purposes compatible with those for which the data were collected. As an agent processing personal information under the direction of its clients, Sony works with its clients so that they can provide a way for individuals to correct their data.

Data Retention

Sony will retain personal information processed on behalf of its clients for as long as needed to provide services to its clients. Sony will retain and use this personal information as necessary to comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements.

Access

As a data processor, Sony has no direct relationship with the individuals whose personal information it processes. Individuals who seek access, or who seek to correct, amend, or delete inaccurate data should direct their query to Sony’s client (the data controller) which has transferred such data to Sony for processing. Sony will respond to its client within 30 business days in response to this request. The client will then provide access to the individual as determined under the applicable local data protection law.

Material Change

Sony may update this Policy to reflect changes to its information practices. If Sony makes any material changes, Sony will update this Policy prior to the change becoming effective.
